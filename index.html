Analog Horror

What is it?

Analog horror is an awesome subgenre of horror that is commonly known for it's VHS looks, often being presented as a series of VHS tapes that contains spooky messages and traditionally has few to none jumpscares. Works are often set in between 1990 and 1950 in order to stay true with the analog era of tape recorders and VCRs and are often related to internet creepypastas. Each of the ones I'll show you have a specific spin on things, some things come from space, some are biblical demons and some... Well we don't know, how about we find out?

Analog horror works I like:

Local 58

Local 58, is a webseries of recordings ripped off an open television broadcast channel on VHS, the channel is based on Mason County, West Virginia callsign WCLV-TV, the recordings show a number of occurrences that the transmission is hijacked by someone or something in order to show ominous, sometimes highly disturbing messages and imagery to the spectators.

Gemini Home Entertainment

This one is also a webseries, this time created by canadian YouTuber called Remy Abode, it's set between 1980 and 1990 and each enty of this series are videos made by various companies ranging from guides to children's cartoons all distributed by, the one and only, Gemini Home Entertainment. This one is great because it incorporates elements of cosmic horror into the usual analog horror mix which blend spectacularly well together!

The Mandela Catalogue

This one is a series created by Alex Kister set in the fictional county of Mandela that depicts the city as being invaded by otherworldy beings nicknamed Alternates that psycologically torture their victims with the end goal of assuming their identities as Doppelgängers. This one contains more biblical themes referencing Noah's Ark, Adam and Eve, the birth of Jesus and so on.

The Backrooms

It's a series of found footage videos made by moviemaker Kane Parsons that bases itself on an old 4chan creepypasta with the same name. Here people seem to fall in or noclip into an unknown place which seems to be the Backrooms of reality itself. It relies heavily on liminal spaces and the uneasy feeling they can cause.
